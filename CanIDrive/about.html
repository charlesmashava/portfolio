Definition of a PWD

What are PWDs?



Persons with Disabilities (PWD): In the context of accessibility and inclusivity, "PWD" often refers to "Persons with Disabilities." This term is used to describe individuals who have physical, sensory, cognitive, or other impairments. It emphasizes the abilities of individuals rather than focusing solely on their disabilities.


There are many different disabilities that are recognized by the Philippines under RA No. 7277. Up to six categories are listed:

Psychosocial disability
Disability caused by chronic illness
Learning disability
Mental disability
Visual disability
Communication disability
Is it even possible?

Can PWDs Drive? Yes you can!

According to the Land Transportation and Traffic Code, R.A. No. 4136, every person who desires to personally operate any motor vehicle needs to file an application to the Director or his deputies for a driver’s license. Once applied and after undergoing the licensure process, the PWD may operate a motor vehicle and comply with the rules and regulations on Philippines roads.


However, not all disabilities will be allowed. Take note of this list of allowable disabilities:

Orthopedically impaired
Polio victims with a paralyzed leg
Partial blindness
Speech and hearing impairment

Only these types of PWDs will be entitled to a driver’s license. Any other PWD like a double amputee, totally blind person or a person with two paralyzed legs will not be allowed to operate a motor vehicle.

PWD Licensure

How can PWDs apply for a license?



The process for PWDs is similar to standard licensure and must go through the process of getting a student permit.

Must be 17-years old or above
Physically and mentally fit to drive
Literate in both English and Filipino
Has completed the mandatory Theoretical Driving Course

Once those prerequisites have been fulfilled, the applicant must now gather his requirements:

Completed student permit application form
Birth Certificate and Marriage Contract (if married)
Original and one photocopy of any issued ID
Medical certificate from an accredited LTO clinic
Certificate of Completion for TDC
Tax Identification Number (TIN)
PWD Licensure

How do I process my application?

1. Head to your preferred LTO licensing center
2. Get a copy of the student permit form and fill in the required information
3. Wait for your number to be called as you will be assigned one with the form
4. Submit your paperwork when called including your other required documents
5. Wait for your picture to be taken and prepare to give your signature as well
6. Head to the cashier for payment and receive your official receipt (OR)
7. Wait again for your name or number to be called then head to the releasing area
8. Present your OR and receive your student permit

Note: You can fill-up your application online with the help of our online registration for LTO.

PWD Licensure

I want to apply for professional or non-professional licenses!



For professional and non-professional licenses, the process and requirements are as follows


Prerequisites:

18 years old and above
Physically and mentally fit
Literate in English and Filipino
Must hold a valid student permit for at least 30 days

Requirements:

Complete Driver's License form
Medical Certificate from LTO
PDC certificate and student permit
Certificate of completion for the 15-hour seminar
PWD Licensure

Professional and Non-professional Licensure Process

1. Head to your preferred LTO licensing center of choice
2. Get a copy of the non-professional driver’s license application form and fill up the required information
3. Wait for your number to be called and submit all of your completed paperwork
4. Wait again as you will be called to have your picture and signature taken
5. Once this is done head to the cashier for payment and make sure to get your OR
6. Wait for your name to be called as this will be done by batch for the written examination for the Basic Driving theory test
7. Once done, wait for your name or number to be called for the practical driving exam
8. Wait again for your name or number to be called as you will head to the claiming area to get your non-professional driver’s license

Car Suggestions for PWDs

Is there a specific car for PWDs?

Not exactly. However, the different types of disabilities can definitely affect the type of vehicle a PWD can use. Some disabilities may also require a car to be modified to fit their capabilities properly.


Customized Vehicles for PWDs

In order to operate a motor vehicle, some PWDs get creative and adapt their space in order to operate their vehicles, and the LTO allows this provided that it meets the standard specifications set and is requested by the owner at the LTO. Following this, the PWD driver’s license holder must only drive the customized vehicle, and it should be noted in their driver’s license.
